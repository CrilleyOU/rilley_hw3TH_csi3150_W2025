Why visit Marquette?

Marquette is a terrific tourist destination for those looking to get away from civilization, without abandoning it all together. Located within the thick forested Upper Peninsula, Marquette is an isolated community of roughly 20,000 living along the shores of Lake Superior. Due to its location, it is surrounded by beautiful natural waterfalls, rock formations, and hardwood forests.

Marquette Hiking and Boat Tour

Regardless of the season, we offer a wonderful way to experience the beauty of Marquette both on land and in water. Your tour begins at the base of Sugarloaf Mountain, where depending on the season, you will have an appropriate mode of transportation to get to the top and take in the surrounding areas. After this we travel down the mountain and board a boat waiting for us to take in Marquette from Lake Superior.

What to Wear

For summer tours, it is recommended that you wear very light, long-sleeved clothing. Marquette's location deep within the northwoods includes lots of black flies and gnats with it. Your tour will provide gloves and masks to wear if your trip is occurring during peak black fly season. For other tours, just dress comfortably for the temperature. Typically it is pretty cool outside of the summers but keep your eye on the forecast.
